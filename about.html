The Story

Built on Service. Driven by Accountability.

Rick Yost didn't start with a financial planning degree or a Wall Street background. He started in the U.S. Army, where you learn fast that unclear plans cost people dearly — and that accountability isn't optional.

That discipline carried into his professional career: over two decades managing complex programs, leading research initiatives, and advising organizations that needed someone who could cut through noise, build a real plan, and hold it accountable.

The financial coaching side came from a pattern he kept seeing — high earners, smart people, good jobs, and still fighting about money. Avoiding the conversation. Letting debt build. One partner stressed, the other checked out. Not because they lacked income. Because they lacked a shared system.

Rick built Yost Group to fix both problems. A consulting firm that operates with military-grade precision. And a financial coaching practice built specifically for couples who are ready to stop the pattern and start building — together.
