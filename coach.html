FAQ

Common Questions

Do we have to be in financial trouble to work with you?
No. Couples come to me at all stages — some are in debt and need a way out, others have solid incomes but can't agree on how to use them, and many just want a clear, shared plan for the future. The common thread is wanting to handle money better as a team.
What's the difference between a financial coach and a financial advisor?
A financial advisor typically manages investments and sells products. A financial coach works with your behavior, habits, and decision-making — the underlying factors that determine whether any plan actually succeeds. I don't sell products. I sell clarity.
What does the 4-month program actually look like?
We meet regularly throughout the four months — mapping your financial picture, building your plan, working through the 10 foundational steps, and adjusting as you make progress. You'll finish with a system you can run on your own.
Is this only for couples, or do you work with individuals too?
My focus is couples. Everything I do is designed for two people navigating finances together. If you're single, I'd encourage you to find a coach whose work is built around that situation — you'll get better results.
Where do sessions take place?
Sessions are conducted via video call, so you can work with me from anywhere. If you're local, in-person may be available — reach out to ask.
